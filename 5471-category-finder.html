Form 5471 Category Finder

Philip Stein & Associates — Internal Tool

Question 1
Are you a US person?
A US person includes a US citizen, US resident alien, domestic corporation, domestic partnership, or domestic trust/estate.
ⓘ
No Filing Requirement
Form 5471 is only required for US persons. Based on your answer, you do not have a Form 5471 filing requirement.
Question 2
Do you have any ownership interest in a foreign corporation?
This includes direct, indirect, or constructive ownership of stock in a foreign corporation.
Question 3
Are you an officer or director of a foreign corporation?
This applies even if you have no ownership interest in the corporation.
ⓘ
No Filing Requirement Indicated
Based on your answers, Form 5471 does not appear to be required. You have no ownership interest and are not an officer or director of a foreign corporation.
Question 3b
Does any US person own 10% or more of the foreign corporation's stock?
By vote or value. This includes any US shareholder, not just you.
ⓘ
No Filing Requirement Indicated
No Form 5471 required based on these facts. Category 2 only applies when a US person owns 10% or more of the foreign corporation.
Question 4
What is your ownership percentage of the foreign corporation?
Include direct, indirect, and constructive ownership combined (by vote or value). Constructive ownership under IRC §318 (as modified by §958(b)) means you may be treated as owning stock held by family members, partnerships, corporations, or trusts you have an interest in. If you are unsure whether attribution applies, consult a tax professional before answering.
Question 5
Did you acquire stock during the tax year that brought you to 10% or more ownership?
This includes any acquisition (purchase, gift, inheritance, etc.) that caused you to meet or exceed the 10% threshold.
Question 6
Did you dispose of stock during the tax year that brought you below 10% ownership?
This includes any disposition (sale, transfer, etc.) that reduced your ownership below the 10% threshold.
Question 7
Do all US shareholders combined own more than 50% of the foreign corporation?
A foreign corporation is a Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC) when US shareholders — counting only those who each own 10% or more (by vote or value, including constructive ownership) — collectively own more than 50% by vote or value. Include all qualifying US shareholders, not just yourself. If you answered "More than 50%" in Q4, the answer here is likely Yes.
Question 8
Does the foreign corporation have at least one US domestic corporation as a 10%+ shareholder?
A Specified Foreign Corporation (SFC) is either (a) a CFC (as determined above), or (b) a foreign corporation in which at least one domestic corporation owns 10% or more (by vote or value). If you answered "Yes" to the previous question (collective US shareholder ownership >50%), this corporation is already an SFC and you can answer Yes. If not, answer based on whether any domestic corporation (not individual) holds ≥10%.
Question 10
Are you an officer or director of this foreign corporation?
If yes, and any US person owns 10% or more, Category 2 may apply.
Question 10b
Does any US person own 10% or more of this foreign corporation's stock?
By vote or value. This includes any US shareholder, not just you.
